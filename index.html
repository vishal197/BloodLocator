Despite being a country with a population of 1.2 billion, India faces a blood shortage of 3 million units. The problem can be addressed if an additional two percent of Indians donated blood, health experts say.

According to a 2012 World Health Organisation (WHO) report, only nine million units are collected annually, while the need is for 12 million units. Blood donation is a crucial problem in our country. The people affected by this are:

  • Infants
  • Soldiers
  • Pregnant Women
  • Emergency accident victims
  • Patients
  • Health Camps
